Emergency Information

In case of emergencies in Washington state, here is a list of important contact numbers and resources related to caregiving and emergency response:

  1. Emergency (Police, Fire, Ambulance): Call 911

    When you call 911, let the operator know the nature of the emergency, your location, and stay on the phone until they provide further instructions or help arrives.

  2. Washington Poison Control Center: 1-800-222-1222

    In case of poisoning or exposure to dangerous substances, this toll-free number is available 24/7. Trained professionals will provide immediate assistance and advice.

  3. Washington State Department of Social and Health Services (DSHS)
    If you require information or resources about caregiving services, contact DSHS:
    - Phone: 1-877-501-2233
    - Website: https://www.dshs.wa.gov/

    DSHS can offer support and guidance for caregiving issues such as elderly care, child care, and disability services.

  4. Adult Protective Services (APS)
    In case of abuse, neglect, or financial exploitation of vulnerable adults, contact APS:
    - Phone: 1-866-363-4276
    - Website: https://www.dshs.wa.gov/altsa/home-and-community-services/adult-protective-services-aps

    APS provides support and protection for vulnerable adults by investigating allegations and ensuring their safety.

  5. Child Protective Services (CPS)
    To report child abuse or for information about children at risk, contact CPS:
    - Phone: 1-866-ENDHARM (1-866-363-4276)
    - Website: https://www.dshs.wa.gov/ca/child-safety-and-protection/report-child-or-vulnerable-adult-abuse

    CPS works to protect children from abuse and neglect.

Keep these numbers readily available and accessible to ensure a prompt response in case of emergencies. It is essential to be prepared, especially in caregiving situations.
